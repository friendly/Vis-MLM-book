## Draft copy

*Visualizing Multivariate Data and Models in R* is a hands-on guide to graphical methods for multivariate data: how to see patterns among many variables at once, and how to visualize, test, and diagnose the multivariate linear models that explain them. Its distinctive contribution is to extend familiar visualization tools for exploration and regression into the multivariate domain, where data ellipses, biplots, and hypothesis–error (HE) plots make the results of MANOVA, multivariate regression, and discriminant analysis directly visible and interpretable. Researchers and graduate students in the social, behavioral, and life sciences who study several response variables together — but analyze them one at a time — will learn how much more their data can tell them. The book assumes only intermediate applied statistics (multiple regression and ANOVA) and basic familiarity with R.

Working through complete R examples with real datasets, you will learn how to:

* explore high-dimensional data with scatterplot matrices, parallel coordinate plots, principal components analysis (PCA), and biplots that reduce many variables to their most informative low-dimensional views;
* understand and diagnose univariate linear models with effect displays, added-variable plots, and influence diagnostics, and tame collinearity through the visualization of ridge regression;
* test and visualize multivariate linear models using Hotelling's T², HE plots, and canonical discriminant plots that show how groups differ on several responses together;
* assess equality of covariance matrices, detect influential observations, and apply robust multivariate estimation.

The complete book is freely available online at https://friendly.github.io/Vis-MLM-book/, including an appendix of R scripts that reproduce nearly every analysis and figure.

**Michael Friendly** is Professor of Psychology at York University, a Fellow of the American Statistical Association, and an associate editor of the *Journal of Computational and Graphical Statistics*. A principal innovator of graphical methods for categorical and multivariate data, he is the author and maintainer of many widely used R packages, including **heplots** and **candisc** featured in this book. His previous books include *Visualizing Categorical Data*, *Discrete Data Analysis with R*, and, with Howard Wainer, *A History of Data Visualization and Graphic Communication* (Harvard University Press).
