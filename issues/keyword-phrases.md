# Keyword phrases for search

Six key words/phrases requested by the editor to improve search results. Per their instruction, none repeat words already in the title (*Visualizing Multivariate Data and Models in R: A Romance in Many Dimensions*), so *visualizing/visualization*, *multivariate*, *data*, *models*, and *R* are all excluded here even though they are natural search terms.

## The six keywords

1. statistical graphics
2. MANOVA
3. principal component analysis
4. biplots
5. regression diagnostics
6. discriminant analysis

## Rationale

- **statistical graphics** — the broadest umbrella term for the book that doesn't reuse "visualization." It is the established discipline name (and the one associated with Michael Friendly's body of work), so it catches readers searching at the field level rather than for a specific method.
- **MANOVA** — Part 4 is "the heart of the book," and MANOVA is the term applied researchers actually type into a search box. Someone searching "MANOVA" is almost never served a book about *visualizing* those analyses — this keyword targets exactly the underserved reader the Preface describes (the researcher examining outcomes one by one).
- **principal component analysis** — the anchor method of Part 2 (@sec-pca-biplot) and one of the highest-volume statistics search terms overall. Spelled out rather than "PCA" because search systems match the acronym within the full phrase, but not always the reverse.
- **biplots** — a distinctive, low-competition term. There are very few book-length treatments that feature biplots prominently, so this keyword gives strong ranking for a term the book genuinely owns.
- **regression diagnostics** — covers the Part 3 material (effect displays, added-variable plots, influence, collinearity, ridge regression) with the phrase practitioners search for. It also signals the "diagnose problems" goal from the book's framework.
- **discriminant analysis** — captures the Chapter 15 material and its multivariate-classification audience. Like MANOVA, it draws in method-first searchers who don't yet know they want a graphics book.

The set was chosen to balance three search intents: field-level browsing (statistical graphics), method-specific queries from applied researchers (MANOVA, principal component analysis, discriminant analysis), and technique terms where the book has few competitors (biplots, regression diagnostics).

## Runner-up choices

- **HE plots** — the book's signature technique, but as a search term it is too niche to spend one of six slots on; those who know the term likely already know Friendly's work. Would be my seventh pick.
- **Hotelling's T²** — precise and relevant (Chapter 10), but lower search volume than MANOVA, which subsumes much of the same audience.
- **canonical correlation** / **canonical discriminant analysis** — covered, but "discriminant analysis" already captures most of this traffic with a more common phrase.
- **ridge regression** / **collinearity** — solid Chapter 9 terms, but narrower than "regression diagnostics," which was kept instead.
- **scatterplot matrix** / **parallel coordinates** — good technique terms from Chapter 4, but individually too specific compared with the field- and method-level terms chosen.
- **effect displays** — useful and distinctive, but the phrase is less standardized in searches than "regression diagnostics."
- **ggplot2** — high search volume, but misleading as a keyword: the book is method-focused, not a ggplot2 tutorial, and mismatched keywords can hurt more than help.
- **dimension reduction** — apt for Part 2, but "dimensions" appears in the subtitle, so it was excluded under the editor's no-repeats rule.
- **exploratory analysis** — natural fit for Part 2, but the full standard phrase is "exploratory data analysis," and "data" is in the title; the truncated form is weaker as a search phrase.
