## Appendix

This appendix presents complete case studies of multivariate data analysis in R, showing how the graphical and statistical methods of the book work together in a real research setting, from research questions through exploration, model fitting, visualization, and model checking. A clinical study asks whether standardized neurocognitive and social-cognitive tests can distinguish patients diagnosed with schizophrenia or schizoaffective disorder from each other and from normal controls, using the NeuroCog and SocialCog datasets from the heplots package. Exploratory displays made with ggplot2 include violin plots and boxplots, a corrgram of the correlation matrix, a scatterplot matrix with data ellipses, and a principal component analysis (PCA) biplot. A multivariate analysis of variance (MANOVA), with contrasts and linear hypotheses testing the research questions, is visualized with hypothesis-error (HE) plots, canonical discriminant analysis, and a canonical HE plot from the candisc package. A chi-square quantile-quantile (QQ) plot of residuals exposes an extreme multivariate outlier, a data entry error that reversed the significance of a key group comparison, demonstrating why graphical model checking matters.
