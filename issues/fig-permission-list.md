# Figure Permission List

This document contains a list of figures that are the property of those other than the author (MF), and thus require permission to use.

**Update (2026-07-08, GK):** entries below have been re-assessed against the publisher's
Permissions guide (`production/Permissions_guide.pdf`, Taylor & Francis). Tagging:

- **[NPR]** = **no permission required**. The justification is appended to the entry in italics
- Untagged entries still require permission or verification — see the consolidated
  section **"Figures requiring permission (updated)"** at the end of this document.

Rules applied from the T&F guide:

1. Public-domain works need no permission (attribution still required; proof of PD status needed).
2. Being available online does **not** make a work public domain — check the actual license tag.
3. Redrawn artwork is still third-party material *unless* "substantially transformed … to make a new work or a new critical point" (guide, footnote 1).
4. "Copyright does not apply to raw data" — a figure generated by MF's own R code from data is an original work, even when the *idea or method* is credited to a blog post or tutorial (ideas and methods are not copyrightable expression).
5. CC-licensed material may be used within its license terms, citing the license as proof.
6. Material MF created *previously* (e.g., figures from his own journal articles) still counts as third-party material — reuse rights must be checked with the original publisher.
